============================================================
PAPER PARAGRAPH
============================================================
To assess cross-domain generalisation using authentic email
content, the fine-tuned BERT model was evaluated on the
Ling-Spam corpus (2,693 test emails) without prior
exposure to this distribution. Zero-shot transfer yielded an
accuracy of 99.22% and F1 score of 99.21%,
confirming distributional shift between corporate email
corpora and the Ling-Spam collection. Few-shot domain
adaptation was then performed using only 200 labelled
Ling-Spam emails over 2 epochs (lr=2x10^-5, AdamW).
Following adaptation, accuracy improved to 99.55%
and F1 to 99.55%, an improvement of
+0.33% and +0.34%
respectively. Unlike the UCI Spambase corpus, Ling-Spam
contains authentic raw email text, ensuring the validity
of transformer-based evaluation without feature
reconstruction approximations.
